Introduction

I started sailing in 2014. The first boat I learned how to sail in is the Sunfish. It is a light, one or two man single sail boat for small racing rigattas. After I Earned my licence in thees boats I moved onto the Flying Scot. The Flying Scot is a larger, cruising boat meant for pleasure sailing.

Sunfish

I have my smallboat sailor certification on this boat and I have raced it many times with a partner in sunfish rigattas.

Flying Scot

At my camp, I was tested on my knowledge of the nomenclature of the flying scot as well as sailing ability in low and high wind days. This icluded live man overboard procedure and high wind 'suicide dockings.' I passed the test on my third attempt, before anyone else in my class and was awarded the rank "Flying Scot Skipper."

Sailing Experience

I was first introduced to sailing in 2014 when I spent my first summer at Camp Sea Gull in Arapahoe, North Carolina. I spent only one week this year in Sea Gull's 'starter camp' program. However, the next year, I spent a whole month learning to sail a small one man boat called the sunfish. That year, I got my Sunfish Skipper rank. In 2016 when I returned, I was able to get my Sunfish Master rank and a Smallboat Sailor liscence with US sailing. This allows me to sail any boat under 15 feet long in the 48 states. Finally, in the summer of 2018 after two more years of sailing, I got my Flying Scot Skipper rank allowing me to check out a Flying Scot. The flying Scot is a Cruising boat that can seat a much larger crew and is fun to sail with your friends. In my 5 years (so far) of sailing experience, I have learned a lot.
